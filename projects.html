Project 01

Wireless Receiver System Design

May 2018 Texas Tech

Designed and simulated a complete multi-stage RF receiver system in AWR including Band-Pass Filters, LNA, Image-Reject Filters, and Mixers. Performed theoretical hand calculations and validated results through AWR simulations.

Key Performance Metrics

  • Noise Figure: 3.985 dB (simulated) vs 3.813 dB (calculated)
  • IIP3: -13.41 dBm with 8 dBm calculation variance
  • SFDR: 41.37 dB for optimized linearity
  • Operating Frequency: 5.8 GHz, 20 MHz BW

Project 02

Design of a PTAT Temperature Sensor Circuit and Layout

Dec 2017 Texas Tech University

Designed a PTAT (Proportional-To-Absolute-Temperature) circuit using AMI06 CMOS process with sub-threshold CMOS transistors to reduce bias voltage and power consumption. Used relatively high gain OP-AMP to keep gate voltages of two subthreshold MOSFETs approximately the same, achieving low line sensitivity.

Design Results

  • Temperature slope: 1040.2 to 1040.7 ppm/°C across supply voltages
  • Supply voltage tested: 1.6V, 2.3V, 3.0V with stable operation
  • Phase margin: 72.59° (3V), 75.47° (2.3V), 120.8° (1.6V)
  • Figure of Merit (FOM): 0.000401

Project 03

Design and Test of an Asynchronous Buck Converter

Dec 2018 Texas Tech University

Designed a voltage-mode buck converter with input filter to reduce input ripple current. Used Type III compensator with MSP430G2235 microcontroller for feedback regulation and PWM control. Circuit was designed, simulated, assembled, and tested for functionality.

Performance Results

  • Efficiency: 93% (exceeding 85% specification)
  • Input: 17-20V, Output: 14V at 1.167A into 12Ω load
  • Phase Margin: 48.9° (target >45°), Gain Margin: 22.4 dB
  • Output ripple: 60 mV, Input ripple: 175 mA
  • Switching frequency: 80 kHz, L=33µH, C=22µF
